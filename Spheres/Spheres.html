Tutotial: Spheres

Prerequisites

Download and install SasView

Learning outcomes

Basic understanding of small-angle scattering

Get intuition about the scattering from spheres and other shapes

Learn simple model fitting in SasView

Learn how interparticle interaction and polydispersity affects the scattering

Part 1: 1Monodisperse spheres

Go to: Shape2SAS, and simulate a sphere with a 50 Å radius

Comment the resulting scattering. Try to compare with a smaller or larger sphere (as Model 2), or with other shapes. Notice the inverse relation: larger particles have features at smaller q.

Download the simulated data: Isim.dat. example data

Load into SasView and fit with sphere model

Does the fitted radius match the input for the simulations (50 Å)?

Part 2: Polydisperse spheres

Go to: Shape2SAS, and simulate a sphere with a radius of 50 Å as Model 1 and a sphere with radius of 50 Å and polydispersity of 0.2 as Model 2.

Comment the results

Note: a gaussian size distribution is used in the simulations, but many different distributions are possible

Part 3: Monodisperse spheres with interparticle repulsion (hard-sphere structure factor)

Go to: Shape2SAS, and simulate a sphere with a radius of 50 Å as Model 1 and a sphere with radius of 50 Å and hard sphere structure factor with volume fraction 0.2 and hard-sphere interaction radius of 50 Å as Model 2.

Comment the results

A hard-sphere structure factor can arise from interparticle repulsion in concentrated samples of dilute identical particles.

Download the simulated data for Model 2: Isim_2.dat. example data

Load into SasView and fit with sphere with hard sphere structure factor

Does the fitted radius, volume fraction and hard-sphere radius match the input for the simulations?
