Learning outcomes

  • Learn to use and interpret pair distance distributions.

Introductory remarks

SAS data is measured in "reciprocal space", i.e. as funciton of 1/length. By a Fourier transformation, one can get a distribution of distances between scattering pairs in the sample (weighted by contrast): the pair distribuion or simply the "p(r)" (colloquially denoted the "p-of-r-function").

The p(r) is in "real space", i.e. as function of length, so it can be interpreted more intuitively than the data. It may provided useful structural information about the sample prior to any modelling.
